7 Essential Questions to Ask Before Hiring an Online Singing Teacher

Finding the right online singing teacher can transform your voice - but choosing the wrong one can waste your time and money. After 25 years of teaching and training other vocal coaches, I have seen what separates great teachers from mediocre ones. Before you book your first lesson, ask these seven essential questions.

1. What is Your Teaching Experience?

Experience matters. A teacher with years of work has encountered hundreds of different voices and knows how to adapt their approach. Ask about their background - not just years teaching, but types of students they have worked with. Have they taught beginners? Professional singers? Different genres?

2. Do You Teach My Style of Music?

A classical opera coach might not be the best fit for someone wanting to sing pop or rock. While good technique transfers across genres, each style has specific nuances. Make sure your teacher understands and enjoys the music you want to sing.

3. What Technical Setup Do I Need?

A professional online singing teacher should have clear requirements for lessons. They need good audio equipment to hear your voice properly, reliable video software, and a system that works for your device. If they say "just use your phone microphone," that is a red flag.

4. Can I See Reviews or Testimonials?

Good teachers have satisfied students. Look for video testimonials, written reviews, or ask if they can provide references. Real results from real students are the best indicator of teaching quality.

5. What is Your Teaching Philosophy?

Some teachers focus on rigid classical technique. Others prioritise performance and confidence. Some are gentle and encouraging; others push you hard. There is no right answer - but there is a right fit for you. Make sure their approach matches your learning style.

6. Do You Offer a Trial Lesson?

Chemistry between teacher and student is crucial. A trial lesson lets you see if you connect, if their teaching style works for you, and if you feel comfortable. Many quality teachers offer discounted or short trial sessions.

7. What Will I Learn in the First Month?

A good teacher has a plan. They should be able to outline what you will cover in your first few lessons, what exercises you will practice, and what progress you can realistically expect. Vague answers suggest they wing it rather than teach systematically.
