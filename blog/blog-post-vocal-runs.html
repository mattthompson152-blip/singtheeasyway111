Mastering Vocal Runs and Riffs

You know the moment. The singer hits a high note, then suddenly releases a cascade of descending notes, each pitch perfect, each note distinct, yet flowing together like water over stones. That is a vocal run.

Vocal runs and riffs are the fireworks of singing. They are technically impressive, emotionally exciting, and when done well, they can be the highlight of a performance.

In my online singing lessons, I teach students how to develop the agility and accuracy needed for clean, professional sounding runs.

Start Slow

The biggest mistake singers make when learning runs is trying to sing them fast immediately. This builds bad habits and muscle memory for inaccuracy.

Instead, break the run down note by note. Sing it slowly, ensuring each pitch is accurate and connected to the next.

The Ga Exercise

One of my favorite exercises for developing clean runs uses the syllable ga. Unlike open vowels that can blur together, ga requires your vocal cords to make distinct, clean engagements for each note.
