Vocal Resonance: Finding Your Best Tone

Have you ever wondered why some singers seem to have voices that fill a room effortlessly? Their tone is rich, warm, and carries without any apparent strain. The secret is resonance.

Vocal resonance is the amplification and enrichment of your voice through the natural resonating chambers of your body. Master resonance, and your voice transforms from thin and weak to full and commanding.

Understanding Resonance

Your voice begins at your vocal cords, but that is just the start. The sound then travels through various spaces in your body, each one adding its own color and amplification.

The main resonators are your throat, your mouth, your nasal cavity, and your chest. Learning to use these spaces effectively is the key to a beautiful tone.

The Mask

One of the most important concepts in resonance is placement in the mask. This refers to the area around your nose, cheekbones, and upper lip. When you feel vibration in this area, you are using your mask resonance effectively.

Try This Exercise

Hum on the sound ng like at the end of the word sing. Feel the buzzing sensation in your nasal cavity and around your face. Now, while maintaining that buzzing feeling, open your mouth to sing ah. The resonance should stay forward and bright.
