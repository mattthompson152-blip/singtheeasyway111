What to Expect From Your First Online Singing Lesson

Taking your first online singing lesson can feel exciting and nerve-wracking at the same time. What will it be like? Do you have the right equipment? Will it actually work? Having taught thousands of students through virtual lessons over the past 8 years, I can tell you that online singing lessons are not just convenient - they are often more effective than traditional in-person sessions.

Before Your Lesson: Setting Up Your Space

You don't need a professional studio. Find a quiet room where you won't be interrupted. Make sure you have:

  • Good lighting: So your teacher can see your posture and facial expressions
  • A stable internet connection: WiFi is fine, but ethernet is better if available
  • A device with a camera: Laptop, tablet, or phone - anything works
  • Water: Keep hydrated throughout the lesson

What Happens in the First 15 Minutes

We'll start with a chat about your goals. Are you preparing for an audition? Want to sing for fun? Looking to expand your range? This helps me tailor the lesson to you. Then we'll do some simple vocal exercises to warm up and assess your current abilities. Don't worry about sounding perfect - this is just to see where you're starting from.

The Technical Stuff (Don't Panic)

Most students worry about audio delay or poor sound quality. Modern video platforms have minimal lag, and I use professional audio routing software that lets me hear your voice clearly. If we have technical issues, we troubleshoot them together. Usually, it's just a matter of adjusting your microphone settings.

What You'll Learn in Lesson One

Every first lesson includes breathing technique basics, a simple vocal warmup routine you can practice daily, and at least one song to work on. You'll leave with homework - specific exercises tailored to your voice. Many students are surprised by how much progress they make in just one session.
